Abstract

Text-driven 3D human motion generation aims to synthesize diverse motion sequences that are semantically aligned with textual descriptions. Existing generative approaches often encode the entire sentence into a single semantic embedding, which compromises both semantic consistency and physical plausibility in the generated motions. To address these limitations, we propose PCDHMG, a novel framework that formulates motion generation as a multiobjective optimization problem. Without introducing additional parameters, PCDHMG enhances both the diversity and quality of the generated motions. Leveraging the semantic understanding capabilities of a large language model (LLM), we decompose the input text into fine-grained behavioral units, each guiding the construction of semantically grounded objective functions. To effectively balance conflicting objectives, we adopt an evolutionary algorithm (EA) augmented by a pretrained generative model to initialize and guide population evolution. Each behavioral unit serves as a semantic anchor, steering the search process toward diverse yet semantically faithful motion sequences. Furthermore, we incorporate physics-based constraints into the optimization process to ensure that the generated motions are smooth, stable, and physically plausible. Experimental results on mainstream human motion datasets demonstrate that our method outperforms existing approaches in terms of both semantic consistency and diversity, providing a robust solution for generating high-quality, text-aligned human motion sequences.

Overview

The overview of the proposed method. The text description segmented by ChatGPT is used to construct a multiobjective optimization function, which is then solved using EA. Pretrained conditional motion generation is employed to initialize the population. A unique physics-based criterion is introduced into the optimization process to ensure the quality of the generated human motions.
